Case Study
A new mobile banking app launches with strong initial downloads. However, analytics show that 70% of new users never open the app again after day 3. Customer support tickets spike around the payment screen, with users reporting they "can't find" the confirm button. A post-launch survey gives the app a satisfaction score of 2.8 out of 5. The product team notices the sign-up completion rate is only 34%, despite industry average being 65%.
1
Which two DUX metrics are most relevant to the problems described? Select the best justification.
A
Satisfaction and error rate, because users are unhappy (2.8/5) and they can't find the confirm button, suggesting they're making errors.
B
Retention rate and satisfaction, since the data shows users are not returning and the survey score of 2.8/5 confirms they are unhappy with the experience.
C
Retention rate and conversion rate — the 70% non-return figure directly measures retention failure, and the 34% sign-up completion versus the 65% industry benchmark is a textbook conversion rate problem, both supported by specific data.
D
Task completion time and heuristic evaluation, because users are struggling to complete tasks and the interface needs an expert review to find where it is going wrong.
2
What do these metrics tell us about the specific DUX problems in the app?
A
The low retention rate shows users do not find enough value to return after initial use. The conversion rate of 34% — well below the 65% industry average — indicates significant usability barriers in the sign-up flow, meaning the app is losing nearly half of all potential users before they even become regular users.
B
The metrics show users are unhappy and don't return, which means the overall DUX is poor and the developers need to fix the app.
C
The retention rate tells us 70% of users leave, which is bad. The conversion rate of 34% also shows people aren't signing up, so both metrics confirm the app has DUX problems that need addressing.
D
The low retention rate suggests the app has performance issues causing users to leave. The conversion rate problem is likely caused by the payment screen being confusing, which stops users from completing sign-up.
3
Which design improvement would best address the metrics identified?
A
Send targeted push notifications to users who haven't opened the app in three days to encourage them to return, directly improving the retention rate metric.
B
Redesign the payment screen to make the confirm button larger and more prominent, which would reduce the number of support tickets and make the app easier to use overall.
C
Conduct a full heuristic evaluation of the app using Nielsen's 10 principles, then address all identified issues to improve both retention and conversion rates.
D
Simplify the sign-up flow by reducing the number of steps and adding a progress indicator. This targets the conversion rate directly by lowering the barrier to completing registration, aiming to close the gap between the current 34% and the 65% industry benchmark.

⚠️ Flawed Interface Description
A hospital appointment booking system has been criticised by patients. When a user submits a booking form, the page simply refreshes with no message — users don't know if their booking was successful. The system uses medical jargon throughout (e.g. "Select your desired consultation modality"). If a user makes an error in the date field, the entire form clears and they must start again with no explanation of what went wrong. There is no undo option. The interface looks completely different depending on whether you're on mobile or desktop. There is no help section, FAQ, or guidance anywhere on the site. Frequent users cannot save preferences or use shortcuts — every booking requires the same 12-step process.
