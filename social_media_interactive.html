Types of Social Media

Ten distinct categories — from social networking to anonymous platforms.

🤝
Social Networking Sites
Facebook · LinkedIn · X
+

Build personal and professional connections by creating profiles, sharing updates and messaging others.

  • Profile creation — personal or professional identity
  • Newsfeed — updates from friends, pages & groups
  • Friend/follow system
  • Private messaging & group chats
  • Groups/Communities and event management
📸
Media Sharing Networks
Instagram · TikTok · YouTube
+

Share photos, videos and other multimedia content with audiences.

  • Upload photos or videos
  • Stories/Reels/Shorts — short-form timed content
  • Filters and editing tools
  • Likes, comments, shares and live streaming
  • Hashtags and tags for discoverability
💬
Discussion Networks
Reddit · Quora · Stack Overflow
+

Ask questions, debate and share opinions in thread-based communities.

  • Q&A format
  • Threaded discussions grouped by topic
  • Upvotes/downvotes — community moderation
  • Subreddits/spaces — specialised interest areas
  • Anonymous or pseudonymous accounts
📌
Bookmarking & Curation
Pinterest · Flipboard · Pocket
+

Discover, save and organise links to content from across the web.

  • Save or "pin" content
  • Collections or boards to organise saves
  • Recommendation algorithms
  • Search and tagging by category
⭐
Consumer Review Networks
TripAdvisor · Yelp · Trustpilot
+

Post reviews and ratings for products, businesses and services.

  • Star ratings and written reviews
  • Photos and videos attached to reviews
  • Business response options
  • Search filters by rating/location
✍️
Blogging Networks
WordPress · Medium · Tumblr
+

Create and share longer-form written content with readers.

  • Text-heavy content — articles, blogs, journals
  • Customisable layouts and themes
  • Followers and comments
  • Tagging and categorising
🛍️
Social Shopping Networks
Depop · Poshmark · Instagram Shop
+

Shop socially by exploring recommendations and influencers' items.

  • In-app purchases
  • Product listings with prices and images
  • Reviews and ratings from buyers
  • Messaging between buyers and sellers
🎯
Interest-Based Networks
Goodreads · Strava · Behance
+

Connect with others who share specific hobbies or professions.

  • Niche communities focused on one interest
  • Activity tracking or sharing
  • Badges or rewards
  • Forums and discussions
🎭
Anonymous Networks
Whisper · Yik Yak · 4chan
+

Share content without revealing your real identity.

  • No user profiles — fully or semi-anonymous
  • Proximity-based posting
  • Short message posting
  • Moderation tools to prevent abuse
🎮
Merged Gaming & Social
Discord · Twitch · Roblox
+

Combine gaming features with social tools like chat and streaming.

  • Voice and video chat in real-time
  • Streaming — share gameplay or live events
  • Servers or communities
  • Emojis, bots and integrations

Functions of Social Media

The key features found across social media platforms.

👤
Profile Setting
Create a personal identity on the platform
▾
  • Username or handle
  • Profile picture and bio
  • Links to websites or other accounts
  • Customisable themes or interests
Example: LinkedIn uses detailed profiles with work history & skills; Instagram uses bios, highlights and links.
📢
Status / Wall Posts
Share updates and thoughts on your timeline
▾
  • Text-based updates
  • Tagging people and locations
  • Scheduled or instant posts
Example: Facebook allows life events posted to a wall; X (Twitter) supports short text-based tweets.
📷
Uploading Media
Share visual and audio content
▾
  • Photos and videos
  • Voice notes and audio clips
  • Reels, stories and short-form content
Example: Instagram is image-focused; TikTok specialises in short-form video uploads.
💬
Messaging
Private or group communication
▾
  • Direct messages (DMs)
  • Group chats
  • Voice/video calling
Example: Instagram and Facebook Messenger offer integrated messaging; Snapchat allows disappearing messages.
🔔
Notifications
Alerts about activity relevant to you
▾
  • Triggered by likes, comments and shares
  • Alerts for new followers or mentions
  • Message notifications
Example: TikTok notifies users when a video goes viral or receives a new comment.
📰
Newsfeeds
Stream of content from connections
▾
  • Algorithm-based or chronological order
  • Ads and sponsored posts included
  • Content from followed accounts and pages
Example: Facebook mixes friend updates with recommended content; X shows trending and "For You" posts.
❤️
Comments & Reactions
Engage with others' content
▾
  • Text comments
  • Emoji reactions and likes
  • Upvotes and shares
Example: YouTube uses thumbs up/down; Facebook offers like, love, laugh, sad, angry reactions.
🔄
Sharing
Repost or forward content to followers
▾
  • Retweets and re-shares
  • Share to stories
  • Direct content sharing via messages
Example: Twitter allows retweets; Instagram lets users share posts to their story or in DMs.
🔖
Saving / Bookmarking
Store content to view later
▾
  • Instagram — "Save to Collection"
  • X (Twitter) — Bookmarks feature
  • Facebook — "Save Post"
Example: Users save recipes or articles to return to without losing them in their feed.
🔐
Privacy & Security Settings
Control who sees your content
▾
  • Public/private account settings
  • Block and report tools
  • Two-factor authentication (2FA)
  • Location tagging controls
Example: Snapchat lets users control who sees stories; TikTok allows private accounts and comment filtering.
🔗
Platform Integration
Connect platforms for easier sharing
▾
  • Post Instagram stories to Facebook automatically
  • Use Google/Apple to sign into LinkedIn
  • Share YouTube videos directly to X or WhatsApp
Example: Cross-platform login (SSO) and automatic cross-posting reduce friction for users.
✏️ Exam Tip

You may be asked to compare features across platforms. Most support profiles, posting and media sharing — but messaging, privacy controls and integration vary by platform purpose.

Legal, Ethical & Professional Implications
🛡️
Data ProtectionLegal
▾

GDPR & Data Protection Act 2018. Organisations must protect personal data. Consent required to store, use or share someone's data.

Example: A company using Facebook Ads must ensure data is lawfully processed under GDPR rules.
📣
LibelLegal
▾

Publishing a false statement that damages someone's reputation. Applies even if you share or retweet a libellous post.

Example: Retweeting a post falsely accusing someone of fraud can be grounds for a libel lawsuit.
👿
Trolling & HarassmentLegal
▾

Provoking or harassing individuals or companies online. May breach UK laws on harassment, malicious communications or hate speech.

Example: Businesses can face fake complaints on review sites; individuals may suffer mental health effects.
🗳️
DisinformationEthical
▾

Spreading false or misleading information to gain an advantage. Raises serious ethical concerns about manipulation and trust.

Example: Fake news campaigns or bot accounts promoting false election narratives.
🚷
Social Isolation & CyberbullyingEthical
▾

Constant use can lead to withdrawal from real-world interactions. Cyberbullying has serious emotional and psychological impacts, especially on young people.

Example: A teen being excluded from group chats or targeted on TikTok.
🔍
Vetting Job ApplicantsProfessional
▾

Employers may research candidates using social media. Raises ethical questions about privacy and may introduce bias.

Example: An inappropriate photo on Instagram may cost someone a job, even if unrelated to the role.
💸
Paid Promotion & Sponsored ContentProfessional
▾

Must clearly mark posts as "ad" or "sponsored". Unethical when promotions are hidden or promote harmful products.

Example: A beauty influencer promoting skincare without disclosing they were paid.
🌍
Power of Large FollowingsEthical
▾

Influencers with millions of followers can bypass traditional media. Can be positive (advocacy) or harmful (harassment, misinformation). Responsibility increases with audience size.

Example: Beyoncé (340M+ followers) can promote work directly to fans without media.
✏️ Exam Tip

"While social media enables outreach and influence, it also carries serious legal, ethical and professional risks such as data misuse, libel, or reputational damage from public backlash."
