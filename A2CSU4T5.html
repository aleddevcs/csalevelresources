Quick Concept Recap

Big Data

Extremely large data sets that can be analysed to reveal patterns, trends, and associations, especially in human behaviour and interactions.

Volume Velocity Variety

Data Mining

The automated analysis of large amounts of data to identify patterns and turn raw data into useful information for decision making.

Example: Police analysing crime patterns in a location.

Predictive Analytics

The use of big data, statistics, and machine learning to predict future behaviour, trends, or likely outcomes.

Example: “People who bought this also bought…”

Data Warehousing

The electronic storage of very large amounts of business data so queries can be run on historical information gathered from commercial activities.

Example: A supermarket storing years of sales records.

Distributed Data

Data is stored across multiple physical locations, servers, or sites rather than being kept in one single central place.

Example: Patient records held at GP surgeries and hospitals.

Distributed Processing

Processing tasks are shared across multiple computers or processors so work can be completed more efficiently.

Example: Different machines processing parts of the same task at the same time.

Final Summary and Knowledge Check

Remember This

  • Big Data is often described using the three Vs: Volume, Velocity, and Variety.
  • Data mining finds useful patterns in large data sets.
  • Predictive analytics uses data to forecast likely future outcomes.
  • Data warehousing stores large amounts of historical business data for analysis.

Key Distributed Systems Ideas

  • Distributed data means the data is stored in more than one location.
  • Distributed processing means the workload is shared across more than one computer.
  • Distributed databases can improve reliability, speed, scalability, and access.

Exam-Style Prompts

1. Explain the difference between distributed data and distributed processing.

2. State two advantages of distributed databases.

3. Give one example of predictive analytics and explain why it fits that term.

1. Distributed data means the storage of data is spread across multiple locations, while distributed processing means computational tasks are shared between multiple computers or processors.

2. Two advantages are improved reliability and faster processing. Other valid answers include better availability, scalability, improved access, and reduced unnecessary data transfer.

3. An online shop recommending “People who bought this also bought…” is predictive analytics because past customer behaviour is being used to predict future purchases.
